KTHumanoid: A robust, 3D-printed, and open-source humanoid robot platform for machine learning research

Code (coming soon) arXiv (coming soon)

Abstract

Commercially available humanoid robots are expensive and not always open and extensible, concerning both hardware and software. In contrast, there is an increased interest in low-cost 3D-printed robots that can be easily extended and reconfigured to meet various research needs. Although a growing number of open-source robots have recently been released, existing platforms are either not completely open or not designed for strength and precision. In this paper, we present KTHumanoid, a completely open-source humanoid platform that is designed specifically for robustness and precision. The design combines 3D printed parts, high-quality servo motors, and bearings, making the 24-degree-of-freedom robot both agile and robust. We evaluate the platform in two competitive settings: inverse kinematics for precise end-effector movements and real-time human imitation, where the robot shadows human motions using a standard 2D camera.
